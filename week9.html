Cardboard Prototype

Overview

This week was about making a form factor for the clock that would incorporate all the design elements from past weeks, and build on the progress from week 7. There were a couple of major considerations:

  1. Components, including clock hands, extra hand, strobe LEDs, status (Neopixel) LEDs, BLE board
  2. Clock Dimensions
  3. Fabrication (Laser cutting)
  4. Circuits
  5. Arduino code
  6. Android app

TLDR ;)

This page shows a lot of my progress, but to save the viewers some time, I'm putting some of the results of this very productive week here.

Components

Redbear Blend

Microcontroller for clock that communicates with phone via BLE. This board has a built in Arduino and is very easy to work with. I control the Neopixels and the strobe lights from the Blend, and the neopixels are powered by the Blend as well. The Blend needs a 5V power supply and can only output a max of 5V, so my 12V strobe lights are powered by an external power source.

See Website

Clock hands

I used real clock hands so that the time moves accurately and this acts like a normal clock too :)

Strobe Light # 1

The light was bright and had the right dimensions, but it directed light on the extra hand from the front and thus covered the most brightly lit. So I dropped it.
Name on Amazon: B-right 12W 6-inch Ultra-thin Round LED Panel Light, 850lm, 80W Incandescent Equivalent, 5000K Cool White

Amazon Link

Strobe Light # 2

Initially I had wanted to use LED strips, but most LED strips weren't bright enough when strobed. Neopixel strip was bright, but since they are individually addressable, their controls are different and thus, it's not possible to strobe them. Finally, I found this bright LED strip. Compared to Strobe Light # 1, this was flexible so I could put it around the inside rim of the clock and focus it on the hand.
Name on Amazon: LE 16.4ft LED Flexible Light Strip, 300 Units SMD 2835 LEDs, 12V DC Non-waterproof, Light Strips, LED ribbon,

Amazon Link

Status Neopixel Lights

I chose the neopixel ring for serveral reasons - it's super bright, has a range of colors, and the ring shape is in harmony with my circular clock. The ring I chose has a diameter of 6.2". The next biggest ring has a diameter of 2.58" and did not create as great of an effect as the bigger one.
Adafruit name: NeoPixel 1/4 60 Ring - 5050 RGB LED w/ Integrated Drivers

Adafruit Website

Extra hand

I think Justin would have killed me if I had used a Lego piece in this one so I used a real clock hand in this one! The benefit of using a real clock hand was that it was weight balanced around the center and thus, the rotational frequency didn't change as the hand was turning around. This was crucial cause even the smallest change in rotational frequency badly impacts the strobe effect.
